# HiROCK

**4D Time-Lapse Photogrammetric Monitoring of Paraglacial Rockfall Dynamics in Rapidly Deglaciating Alpine Terrain**

## Objectives

HiROCK establishes an autonomous multi-camera monitoring system capable of producing high-resolution four-dimensional datasets of rockwall activity in recently deglaciated alpine terrain. By combining time-lapse imaging with photogrammetric reconstruction, the system enables detection and quantification of individual rockfall events at sub-daily temporal resolution. The project provides new insights into the frequency, magnitude, and environmental drivers of rockfall activity in paraglacial rockwalls.

## Relevance

Rapid glacier retreat is exposing formerly ice-buttressed slopes to altered thermal and hydrological regimes, leading to increased rockfall activity and rock slope instability. Despite the growing recognition of these processes as hazards for alpine infrastructure, tourism, and communities, quantitative observations of rockfall dynamics in recently deglaciated environments remain limited. Existing methods (episodic field surveys, repeat TLS, multi-temporal airborne surveys) lack the temporal resolution to resolve individual event timing and environmental triggers.

## Study Area

Schwarze Wand, Gepatschferner, Kaunertal, Tyrol, Austria — a recently deglaciated rockwall with active paraglacial adjustment.

## Methods

- Autonomous multi-camera time-lapse network for continuous rockwall monitoring
- Multi-view photogrammetric 3D reconstruction from time-lapse imagery
- Automated change detection for identifying individual rockfall events
- Analysis of event frequency, magnitude, and environmental triggers (temperature, precipitation, freeze–thaw cycles)
- High-resolution 4D surface change datasets
